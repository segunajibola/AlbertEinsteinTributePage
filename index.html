Albert Einstein

The man who was regarded as one of the greatest physicists of all time.

Here's a time line of Albert Einstein's life:

  • 1879 - Born: 14 March 1879, Ulm, Germany.
  • 1891 - Talmud introduced him to a children’s science series by Aaron Bernstein, Naturwissenschaftliche Volksbucher (1867–68; Popular Books on Physical Science).
  • 1894 - Einstein’s education was disrupted by his father’s repeated failures at business after his company failed to get an important contract to electrify the city of Munich.
  • 1896 - He graduated from a special high school run by Jost Winteler in Aarau, Switzerland.
  • 1902 - He and Mileva had a child, Lieserl.
  • 1903 - Einstein married Mileva.
  • 1905 - Einstein’s papers were ignored by the physics community.
  • 1911 - Einstein applied directly to the Eidgenössische Polytechnische Schule (“Swiss Federal Polytechnic School”).
  • 1913 - He started serving as director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics (although the opening of the University of Berlin the institute was delayed until 1917).
  • 1919 - He divorced Mileva and he agreed to give her the money he might receive if he ever won a Nobel Prize.
  • 1915 - Einstein finally completed the general theory of relativity, which he considered to be his masterpiece and gave six two-hour lectures at the University of Göttingen
  • 1930 - His son Eduard was diagnosed with schizophrenia and suffered a mental breakdown.
  • 1931 - One Hundred Authors Against Einstein was published.
  • 1932 - Einstein decided to leave Germany forever.
  • 1933 - Einstein’s close friend, physicist Paul Ehrenfest, who helped in the development of general relativity, committed suicide.
  • 1936 - Einstein’s beloved wife, Elsa, died in 1936.
  • 1939 - Physicist Leo Szilard convinced Einstein that he should send a letter to U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt urging him to develop an atomic bomb.
  • 1935 - Einstein was granted permanent residency in the United States and became an American citizen in 1940, although he chose to retain his Swiss citizenship.
  • 1905 - Einstein handwritten copy of his paper on special relativity was sold for $6.5 million. It is now located in the Library of Congress.
  • 1935 - Einstein’s most celebrated attack on the quantum theory led to the EPR (Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen) thought experiment.
  • 1950 - He published an article on his theory in Scientific American, but because it neglected the still-mysterious strong force, it was necessarily incomplete.
  • 1993 - A Nobel Prize was awarded to the discoverers of gravitation waves, predicted by Einstein.
  • 1995 - Albert Einstein died.
